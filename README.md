
# Dark Energy Brane Simulation

### **Abstract**
This simulation models a cyclical multiverse framework where "Dark Energy" is not an intrinsic cosmological constant $\Lambda$, but rather a residual kinetic ripple within our spacetime fabric. This ripple is generated by a transient, higher-dimensional gravitational coupling event (a "Big Bounce") with a neighboring parallel universe. 

---

### **Graph 1: Spacetime Dynamics During the Coupling Event**
The first plot tracks the spatial positions (expansion/contraction vectors) of two independent cosmic systems over a timeline of 60 arbitrary units.

* **Pre-Collision Isolation ($t = 0$ to $t = 10$):** Initially, Our Universe ($\omega_1 = 1.0$) and the Neighboring Universe ($\omega_2 = 1.2$) fluctuate smoothly at their native resonant frequencies. Because they possess distinct internal frequencies, they remain completely decoupled, representing two isolated cosmic membranes ("branes") moving independently through higher-dimensional bulk space.
* **The Collision/Bounce Phase ($t = 15$ to $t = 25$):** As the systems approach a critical proximity boundary, a time-dependent Gaussian interaction takes effect. During this window, the positions of both universes become highly non-linear and chaotic. The sharp, erratic spikes represent the violent exchange of kinetic energy and mutual gravitational repulsion as the two boundaries compress against one another.
* **Post-Collision Relaxation ($t = 30$ onward):** Following the interaction, the universes separate and return to a stable state. Crucially, however, Our Universe (the blue line) does not return to its initial geometric pattern. The baseline rhythm has been permanently altered, visually demonstrating that a significant amount of external energy was dumped into our system during the bounce.
